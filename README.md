📌 Project Overview

The Google Play Store is one of the world's largest mobile application marketplaces, hosting millions of apps across various categories. This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Google Play Store Apps dataset to uncover patterns related to app categories, ratings, installs, pricing, content ratings, and genres.

The objective is to understand user preferences, identify factors contributing to app popularity, and generate actionable insights for developers and businesses.

🎯 Objectives

Analyze the distribution of applications across categories.

Identify categories with the highest number of installs.

Explore app ratings and user review patterns.

Compare Free and Paid applications.

Examine pricing trends among paid apps.

Analyze content ratings and target audiences.

Identify the most common app genres.

Generate business insights through data visualization.

🔍 Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

The following preprocessing steps were performed:

Checked dataset structure, dimensions, and data types.

Identified and handled missing values.

Removed duplicate application records.

Converted Rating and Reviews columns into numeric format.

Cleaned the Installs column by removing commas and "+" symbols.

Converted Installs into numerical format.

Cleaned the Price column by removing "$" symbols.

Converted Price into numeric format for analysis.

Verified data consistency before visualization.
